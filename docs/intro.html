The U.S. COVID-19 Atlas

In a quickly changing pandemic landscape, access to state and county-level data with rapid regional analysis is crucial to understanding needs and supporting planning efforts. County-level visualizations show a dramatically more detailed pandemic landscape, as using aggregate data alone can miss local hotspots of surging COVID cases.


This atlas is in constant development by the Center for Spatial Data Science at the University of Chicago, with collaboration from the UW-Madison Data Science Institute. County-level data sources include 1P3A, Covidtracker, and USAFacts (see more).

COVID Clusters

We show COVID-19 hot spots two ways: using the total number of confirmed cases, and then by adjusting for population. Because of the infectious nature of COVID, high numbers of cases anywhere will be of concern. At the same time, identifying areas that have a disproportionately high number of cases within the population is needed to locate areas hardest hit by the pandemic. More Details

On the Map:

Click on the Data you're interested (eg. confirmed cases per 1 million persons), and then click on Local Clustering.
Hot spot clusters appear red.


Emerging Risk

Clusters are groups of counties that have a high number of cases, neighbored by counties with a high number of cases. Outliers are areas that have a high number of cases within the county and fewer cases in neighboring counties, highlighting an emerging risk or priority for containment. More Details

On the Map:

Click on the Data you're interested (eg. confirmed cases per 1 million persons), and then click on Local Clustering.
Spatial outliers appear pink.
