The US Covid Atlas

The US COVID Atlas is a county-level visualization tool developed by a University of Chicago-led research coalition that enables interactive exploration of COVID case data and community indicators across the United States from multiple sources, from the beginning of the pandemic to today. In a quickly changing pandemic landscape, access to county-level data and regional insights is crucial to understanding needs and supporting planning efforts to minimize the impacts of the COVID-19 Pandemic.


Explore the Atlas




Near Real-Time Exploration of the Pandemic

Statistical hotspots of confirmed cases of COVID-19 per 10,000 persons, shown from March to yesterday with USAFacts data.

The Atlas is updated by 3pm each afternoon with the latest available data. We recommend a desktop or larger screen for optimal viewing.






Features





Regional Hotspots

Statistical clusters of both confirmed and daily new COVID cases & deaths, with and without population adjustment.



Change Over Time

Watch the spread of accumulated or daily new COVID cases using a time slider and live time-animation.



Interactive Exploration

Uncover trends of the pandemic by comparing different data sources, variables, and spatial analytic insights.



Linked Community Data

Connect to relevant social, economic, and health indicators to provide meaningful community context.






Usage #1

Identify Regional Hotspots for Mitigation

We show COVID-19 hot spots two ways: 1) using the total number of confirmed cases, and 2) adjusting for population. Because of the infectious nature of COVID, high numbers of cases anywhere will be of concern. At the same time, identifying areas that have a disporotionately high number of cases within the population is necessary to locate areas hit hardest by the pandemic.
