Methodology

Data:

To get early access to county level data, our team leveraged crowdsourced county-level estimates from the 1point3acres tracker, a dataset generated by a team of volunteers led by an Uber technical lead since the beginning of the epidemic. The dataset is validated daily against state health department estimates. We recently joined a regional effort to amplify impact with UW-Madisonâ€™s COVID Data Science team, led by Brian Yangell, to further validate these county estimates against Coronaracker, USAFacts, and state department daily reports.


Interactive Exploration:

COVID cases are commonly visualized across multiple dashboards as quantile choropleth maps or graduated points around state centroids. County-level visualizations are rare but when viewed, show a dramatically more nuanced and detailed pandemic landscape. We visualize data at both state and county-scale as total cases, deaths, and population-weighted rates to provide a richer understanding of the pandemic. Case information can be explored by clicking on county or state areas to generate pop-up windows, or to change graphs of confirmed cases.
